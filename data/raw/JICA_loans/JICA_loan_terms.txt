JICA ODA LOAN TERMS — BENGALURU METRO RAIL PROJECT (PHASE 3) (I)
=================================================================
Source: JICA Press Release (March 24, 2026)
Press page: https://www.jica.go.jp/english/information/press/2025/20260324_11.html
Ex-Ante Evaluation PDF: https://www.jica.go.jp/english/information/press/2025/__icsFiles/afieldfile/2026/03/24/jizen_ENG_BengaluruMetro.pdf
Loan Agreement Date: March 24, 2026
Signatories: Joint Secretary Alok Tiwari (DEA, Ministry of Finance, GoI)
             Chief Representative Takeuchi Takuro (JICA India)

FINANCIAL TERMS
---------------
Maximum Loan Amount  : JPY 102,480 million (¥102,480M)
Interest Rate        : Project component: TORF + 80 bp
                       Consulting Services: 0.80% p.a.
Repayment Period     : 30 Years
Grace Period         : 10 Years
Estimated Total Project Cost: JPY 305,331 million

BORROWER / LOCATION
-------------------
Borrower  : Republic of India
Location  : Bengaluru Metropolitan Region, State of Karnataka

PROJECT SCOPE
-------------
Project: Bengaluru Metro Rail Project (Phase 3) (I)

Lines financed:
  Line 3-1: 32.15 km, 22 stations (all elevated)
  Line 3-2: 12.50 km, 9 stations (all elevated)

Eligible categories financed by JICA ODA loan:
  a) Civil and architectural works (Line 3-1 and Line 3-2) [partial]
  b) Trackwork and turnouts
  c) Electrical and mechanical works for stations, rolling stock depots, substations
  d) Automatic Fare Collection (AFC) system
  e) Signaling and train control system
  f) Communication system
  g) Rolling stock [partial]:
       Line 3-1: 192 cars (32 trainsets × 6-car formation)
       Line 3-2: 60 cars (10 trainsets × 6-car formation)
  h) Rolling stock depots (2 locations) — NOT funded by JICA
  i) Consulting services (design review, bidding assistance, construction supervision)

PROJECT OBJECTIVE
-----------------
To cope with increasing traffic demand in Bengaluru Metropolitan Region by expanding
the mass rapid transportation system, thereby promoting regional economic development,
improving urban environment, and mitigating climate change through relief of traffic
congestion and reduction of motor vehicle pollution.

CONTEXT
-------
- Bengaluru population projected to reach ~32 million by 2051
- Traffic congestion causes ~¥69.3 billion annual economic loss (CMPB)
- PM2.5 levels 6.5× WHO standard (2019, IQAir)
- 421 Japanese company bases in Bengaluru (2nd-highest in India, October 2024)
- City ranked 499th/501 most congested globally (TomTom Traffic Index 2024)

NOTE ON INTEREST RATE
---------------------
TORF = Tokyo Overnight Risk-Free Rate (Japanese equivalent of SOFR/SONIA)
TORF+80bp is a floating-rate structure. No fixed rate was published in the press release.
The project component for the concurrent Punjab Irrigation project (also signed Mar 2026)
used a fixed 2.90% rate for comparison.
